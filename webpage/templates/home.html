Why is this project necessary?


Over the past few years, the popularity of organic and eco-friendly food products has increased, resulting in a higher demand and wideravailability of such products. This shift in consumer preferences can be attributed to a growing interest in natural diets that are free from artificial colors, preservatives, additives, and packaging that can harm the environment. Vegetarian and vegan diets have also played a significant role in promoting this movement and driving the consumption of organic products. However, despite the growing trend, young people and students, who are often the main consumers of such products, still face food and economic challenges. According to the National Observatory of Student Life (OVE), the COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated these issues, leading to new inequalities among students. A study found that the average French food basket has increased by 16.3% between January 2022 and January 2023,with 56% of students reportedly experiencing food insecurity.


Our purpose


Our project aims to create a communal urban garden where a variety of fruits and vegetables are grown using organic and ecological methods, without the use of chemical herbicides, fertilizers, or pesticides. The produce from the garden is sold at an affordable price, catering to those with economic difficulties who are looking to improve their food quality, including students. The project also incorporates cutting-edge technology, including a mobile app, website, and image recognition payment system to make it more appealing and modern. This innovative solution approaches the food and economic crisis by promoting the sale of healthy and natural food at a low cost also being environmentally beneficial.
